About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the College of Criminology and Criminal Justice at Florida State University. My research interests focus on why some areas in cities have more crime than others. Specifically, I am interested in spatial crime patterns, what drives these patterns, and how these patterns change over time by investigating spatial attributes of physical environments and structural characteristics within and around the areas. In my research, I build on these interests by employing sophisticated Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and quantitative research techniques as well as interdisciplinary theoretical frameworks drawing from criminology, urban sociology, public health, and geography.

Teaching

Crime Hotspots (Undergrad)

The student will obtain an understanding of: 1) Perspectives on the spatial variation on crime; 2) Situational and spatial explanations; 3) The historical development of the ecological explanations of crime; 4) Significant milestones in the development of place-level explanations of crime

  • Syllabus (Github)

Individual and Society (Undergrad)

The student will obtain an understanding of: 1) The historical development of cities; 2) Theories of urban space and the theoretical roots of modern and future cities; 3) Significant milestones and spatial explanations in the development of cities ; 4) Perspectives on the variation on crime in cities: Situational and community explanations; 5) Ecological perspectives on spatial patterns of crime in cities; 6) The range of spatially explicit crime intervention and prevention

  • Syllabus (Github)

Introduction to Crime Analysis (Undergrad)

Upon completion of the course, students will be able to: 1) Describe the role of the crime and intelligence analyst in law enforcement; 2) Discuss the concepts, theories, practices, data, and analytic techniques used by crime analysts; 3) Analyze crime patterns using multiple sources of crime data; 4) Apply the SARA model of decision-making to solve crime problems and evaluate responses to crime; 5) Describe and evaluate the technologies used for crime and intelligence analysis, and the role of Real Time Crime Centers
