About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the College of Criminology and Criminal Justice at Florida State University. My research interests focus on why some areas in cities have more crime than others. Specifically, I am interested in spatial crime patterns, what drives these patterns, and how these patterns change over time by investigating spatial attributes of physical environments and structural characteristics within and around the areas. In my research, I build on these interests by employing sophisticated Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and quantitative research techniques as well as interdisciplinary theoretical frameworks drawing from criminology, urban sociology, public health, and geography.
